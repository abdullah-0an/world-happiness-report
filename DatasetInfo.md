# World Happiness Report 2024 Dataset

This dataset contains detailed metrics on well-being and happiness indicators, derived from the World Happiness Report 2024. It spans multiple years and includes various dimensions of happiness and quality of life. The data provides a comprehensive view of factors that contribute to life satisfaction, economic prosperity, and social well-being across different countries.

## Dataset Overview

The dataset includes a variety of features that reflect subjective well-being, quality of life, and societal factors that influence happiness. These features are reported for each country and year, enabling detailed analysis of trends and patterns in global well-being.

### Features

- **Country name**: The country for which the data is reported.
- **Year**: The year in which the data was collected.
- **Life Ladder**: A measure of subjective well-being or life satisfaction on a scale where higher values generally indicate greater happiness.
- **Log GDP per capita**: The logarithm of GDP per capita, reflecting economic prosperity and its impact on happiness.
- **Social support**: A metric indicating the level of perceived social support or network available to individuals.
- **Healthy life expectancy at birth**: The number of years a person is expected to live in good health from birth.
- **Freedom to make life choices**: A measure of how free individuals feel in making life decisions.
- **Generosity**: A metric reflecting the level of generosity or charitable giving in a country.
- **Perceptions of corruption**: A measure of how corrupt the government is perceived to be, influencing trust and satisfaction.
- **Positive affect**: The level of positive emotions such as joy and contentment experienced by individuals.
- **Negative affect**: The level of negative emotions such as sadness and anxiety experienced by individuals.

## Usage

This dataset is valuable for analyzing the relationships between various factors and overall happiness, exploring temporal changes, and comparing well-being metrics across different countries and years. It can be used for various types of analysis, such as:

- **Trend Analysis**: Examining temporal changes in life satisfaction and happiness indicators.
- **Country Comparisons**: Comparing well-being metrics across different countries and regions.
- **Correlation Analysis**: Exploring how economic factors (e.g., GDP, freedom, generosity) correlate with life satisfaction and other happiness indicators.
- **Impact Assessment**: Assessing the impact of social support, health, and corruption on overall well-being.

### Example Use Cases

- **Research**: Academic researchers can explore the factors that influence happiness, conducting statistical analysis or predictive modeling.
- **Policy Makers**: Governments and organizations can use this data to design better policies aimed at improving life satisfaction and social well-being.
- **Data Science Projects**: Data scientists can use the dataset to create predictive models or visualizations that explain trends in happiness or identify factors that influence quality of life.

## File Format

The dataset is provided in CSV format, which can easily be loaded into various data analysis tools such as Python (using Pandas), R, Excel, or other statistical software.

## Data Source

This dataset is derived from the World Happiness Report 2024, an annual publication by the United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network (SDSN). The report compiles data from various sources, including Gallup World Poll, and measures factors that contribute to subjective well-being and happiness.
